Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)

The following parameters are indicative of elevated LV filling pressures in patients with HCM (Table 7):

  • Average E/e' > 14
  • Ar-A duration ≥ 30 ms
  • Peak TR velocity > 2.8 m/s
  • LA maximum volume index > 34 mL/m²

Restrictive Cardiomyopathy (RCM)

The following parameters are indicative of elevated LV filling pressures in patients with RCM (Table 7):

  • Average E/e' > 14
  • Deceleration Time (DT) < 140 ms
  • E/A ratio > 2.5
  • IVRT < 50 ms

LV Assist Device (LVAD)

The following parameters are indicative of elevated LV filling pressures in patients with an LVAD (Table 7):

  • E/A ratio > 2
  • RAP > 10 mm Hg
  • PASP > 40 mm Hg
  • Average E/e' ratio > 14 or Septal E/e' ratio ≥ 15
  • LA maximum volume index > 33 mL/m²
  • Interatrial septum position (bulging to right)

Sinus Tachycardia

The following parameters are indicative of elevated LV filling pressures in patients with Sinus Tachycardia (Table 7):

  • Predominant early LV filling pattern with depressed LVEF
  • IVRT ≤ 70 ms (specific)
  • Pulmonary vein systolic filling fraction ≤ 40% (specific)
  • Average E/e' ratio > 14 (high specificity, low sensitivity)
  • Note: Post-PVC beats can help separate fused E and A waves.

Mitral Regurgitation (MR)

The following parameters are indicative of elevated LV filling pressures in patients with Mitral Regurgitation (Table 7):

  • IVRT < 60 ms
  • Ar-A duration ≥ 30 ms
  • IVRT/TE-e' ratio < 5.6
  • Average E/e' ratio > 14 (in patients with depressed EF)

Mitral Stenosis (MS)

The following parameters are indicative of elevated LV filling pressures in patients with Mitral Stenosis (Table 7):

  • IVRT < 60 ms
  • Mitral A peak velocity > 1.5 m/s
  • IVRT/TE-e' ratio < 4.2
